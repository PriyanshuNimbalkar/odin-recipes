Bhaji

Bhaji is a general term for vegetable preparations in Maharashtra. Batata Bhaji is a simple, spiced potato stir-fry, while Bhogi Chi Bhaji is a festive mixed vegetable curry made with winter vegetables and a nutty, aromatic paste. Both are commonly served with puris or chapatis.

Ingredients to make

  • 4 medium potatoes (boiled, peeled, and chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon urad dal (optional)
  • 1 cup chopped onions
  • 1–2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 10–12 curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  • 1/3 cup water

Steps to make

  1. Heat oil in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them pop, then add cumin seeds and urad dal.
  2. Add onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add green chilies, ginger, and curry leaves. Sauté briefly.
  4. Add turmeric and asafoetida. Mix well.
  5. Add chopped potatoes and salt. Stir to combine.
  6. Add water, cover, and cook for 5–7 minutes.
  7. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot.
