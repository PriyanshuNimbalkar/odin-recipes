Amti

Amti is a classic Maharashtrian dal preparation made with toor dal (split pigeon peas), flavored with goda masala, jaggery, tamarind or kokum, and a tempering of mustard seeds, cumin, curry leaves, and green chilies. It is known for its balanced sweet, tangy, and mildly spicy flavors, and is typically enjoyed with steamed rice and a dollop of ghee.

Ingredients to make Amti

  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas/arhar dal)
  • 2 cups water (for pressure cooking dal)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 tablespoon oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • Pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 1 sprig curry leaves
  • 2 green chilies, chopped
  • 1 tomato, chopped
  • 1 tablespoon goda masala
  • 1/2 to 1 tablespoon jaggery
  • 3–4 kokum petals or 2 teaspoons tamarind paste
  • 2 tablespoons grated fresh coconut (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • Finely chopped coriander leaves for garnish

Steps to make Amti

  1. Rinse and pressure cook toor dal with turmeric and water until soft. Mash and set aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter, then add cumin seeds, asafoetida, curry leaves, and green chilies.
  3. Add chopped tomato and sauté for 1–2 minutes.
  4. Add goda masala and (optional) coconut. Mix well.
  5. Add the cooked dal and water to adjust consistency. Stir to combine.
  6. Add kokum or tamarind paste, jaggery, and salt. Mix well.
  7. Simmer for 5–10 minutes to blend flavors.
  8. Garnish with coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or chapati.
